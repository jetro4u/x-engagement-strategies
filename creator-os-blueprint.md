---

# 1. Core Philosophy Layer (Must Anchor Everything)

Before structure, define the governing principle:

> **Posts do not go viral. Behaviors do.**

Your system must therefore optimize for **behavioral triggers**, not formats.

### Three Laws of Creator Physics

### **Law 1 — Attention Precedes Persuasion**

If the scroll doesn’t stop, nothing else matters.

### **Law 2 — Emotion Drives Interaction**

People respond when they feel something:

* status
* curiosity
* surprise
* belonging
* validation
* tension

### **Law 3 — Ease Multiplies Response**

The less thinking required to reply, the higher the engagement velocity.

Many creators violate this unknowingly.

# 2. System Architecture (The Stack)

This is the cleanest structural synthesis of everything discussed.

## The Behavioral Post Stack

```
Layer 1 — Pattern Interrupt
Layer 2 — Cognitive Structure

Layer 4 — Value Density
Layer 5 — Response Engineering
Layer 6 — Network Signaling
Layer 7 — Algorithmic Momentum
```

If you build the AI later, these become modules.

---

## Layer Definitions

### **Layer 1 — Pattern Interrupt**

Primary function: stop the thumb.

Sources:

* contrast
* contrarianism
* shock
* curiosity gap
* split sentence

Failure here = invisible post.

---

### **Layer 2 — Cognitive Structure**

How quickly the brain understands the post.

Use:

* lists
* triads
* symmetry
* definitions
* transformations

Readable posts outperform intelligent but dense posts.

---

### **Layer 3 — Emotional Trigger**

The psychological ignition.

Derived heavily from the 12 prompts.

Top performers:

* vulnerability
* prediction
* identity
* recognition
* humor
* challenge

No emotion → no reaction.

---

### **Layer 4 — Value Density**

“Was this worth my attention?”

Signals:

* teaches something
* reframes something
* simplifies something
* reveals something

Low value posts may get likes…

…but rarely get follows.

---

### **Layer 5 — Response Engineering** *(Extremely High Leverage)*

Design the reply before publishing.

Methods:

* binary questions
* identity questions
* prediction prompts
* low-effort opinions
* “agree or disagree”
* fill-in-the-blank

### Golden Rule:

> Never force the reader to invent the reply.

---

### **Layer 6 — Network Signaling**

This is the hidden growth driver most creators never model.

Signals include:

* replies
* quote tweets
* saves
* profile clicks
* follower overlap

The algorithm reads these as:

> “This creator is worth routing traffic to.”

---

### **Layer 7 — Algorithmic Momentum**

Momentum builds when posts trigger:

* fast early engagement
* discussion depth
* return visits

Momentum is why some average posts explode.

And great ones sometimes don’t.

# 4. The Creator Decision Engine (Pre-Post Checklist)

This becomes extremely valuable later for scoring.

### Ask BEFORE publishing:

**Attention**

* Would this stop *you* mid-scroll?

**Clarity**

* Can it be understood in under 2 seconds?

**Emotion**

* What will the reader feel?

**Response**

* Is replying effortless?

**Identity**

* Does sharing this make the reader look smarter?

**Novelty**

* Have people seen this framing before?

If 2+ answers feel weak → improve before posting.

# 5. Content Operating Modes

This is where most creators lack intentionality.

You should not always post for the same outcome.

## The Four Modes:

### **1. Growth Mode**

Designed for reach.

Use:

* contrast
* extremes
* predictions
* callouts

---

### **2. Authority Mode**

Designed for respect.

Use:

* intellectual clarity
* open-source insights
* frameworks
* deep explanations

Authority converts followers into believers.

---

### **3. Relationship Mode**

Designed for loyalty.

Use:

* vulnerability
* recognition
* community references

This builds defensibility.

---

### **4. Momentum Mode**

Designed to wake the algorithm.

Use:

* questions
* debates
* contrarian takes

Rotate modes deliberately.

Random posting = random growth.
